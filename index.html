FlyBox — Circadian Rhythm Instrumentation

Designed and fabricated programmable light/dark-cycle enclosures for Drosophila circadian rhythm research under Prof. Schlichting. Each unit is a 17-panel laser-cut acrylic enclosure integrating IR/RGB LEDs, cooling fans, and camera modules, controlled by an ESP32 microcontroller programmed via Arduino IDE.

Responsibilities spanned the full hardware stack: enclosure design, fabrication on a Bosslaser LS-1420 CO₂ cutter and Prusa Mini+ FDM printer, component assembly, and firmware development for environmental monitoring and programmable light scheduling.

Biomechanics Design Challenge · Coursework

Hybrid HTO Fixation Plate — Biomechanical Design

Designed a novel high tibial osteotomy (HTO) fixation plate in Ansys Discovery, synthesizing features from three commercial implants (ContourLock, iBalance, TomoFix) into a single optimized geometry. Material: Ti-6Al-4V. Final dimensions: 64 mm length, 22 mm head, 2.5 mm thickness, curvature introduced via split-and-rotate CAD operation, three 4 mm locking screw holes.

FEA in Ansys Discovery yielded a maximum Von Mises stress of 773 MPa and a factor of safety of 1.09 against the yield strength of Ti-6Al-4V (880 MPa). The design was iteratively optimized to remove material from low-stress regions while maintaining FOS above the required minimum of 1.05, resulting in a final mass of 20.52 g and a material cost of ~$0.35 USD.
