FlyBox — Circadian Rhythm Instrumentation

Designed and fabricated programmable light/dark-cycle enclosures for Drosophila circadian rhythm research under Prof. Schlichting. Each unit is a 17-panel laser-cut acrylic enclosure integrating IR/RGB LEDs, cooling fans, and camera modules, controlled by an ESP32 via Arduino IDE.

Responsibilities spanned the full hardware stack: enclosure design, fabrication on a Bosslaser LS-1420 CO₂ cutter and Prusa Mini+ FDM printer, component assembly, and firmware for environmental monitoring and programmable scheduling.

Hybrid HTO Fixation Plate — Biomechanical Design

Designed a novel high tibial osteotomy (HTO) fixation plate in Ansys Discovery, synthesizing features from three commercial implants (ContourLock, iBalance, TomoFix) into a single optimized geometry. Material: Ti-6Al-4V. Dimensions: 64 mm length, 22 mm head, 2.5 mm thickness, three 4 mm locking screw holes.

FEA yielded a maximum Von Mises stress of 261 MPa and a factor of safety of 3.24, cross-validated analytically in MATLAB (347 MPa / FOS 2.54), reconciled by a stress concentration factor of ~1.5 at the screw holes.
